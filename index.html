About Me

I am Ramya, pursuing my Master of Science degree in Computer Science at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC). Prior to this, I worked as a Software Engineer at Razorpay, a unicorn startup in India.

I am also a research volunteer at PathCheck, an MIT Media Lab spin-off, and a research collaborator at the University of Cambridge. My academic and professional journey has revolved around my fervent interest in designing and building robust distributed systems, versatile general-purpose computing solutions, and efficient storage systems.

I completed my Bachelor's in Computer Science at the National Institute Of Technology Karnataka, Surathkal, in 2020. My main research interests are in Distributed and Storage Systems, and you can explore my projects on my GitHub.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I am actively involved in personal projects, such as the ongoing "Distributed Serverless GNN Training Framework System," which leverages serverless computing for efficient Graph Neural Network (GNN) training. I have also made contributions to projects like "Exploiting Nil Externality in message queues" and "Syros - Read Optimized Consensus Protocol," demonstrating my dedication to pushing the boundaries of technological solutions within the dynamic realm of distributed systems.

My overarching career goal is to continue contributing my skills and passion to the design and development of cutting-edge distributed systems, versatile storage solutions, and high-performance general-purpose computing solutions, with a focus on solving challenging problems related to scalability, consensus, storage, memory, and fault tolerance. These areas closely align with my core interests and areas of expertise.
